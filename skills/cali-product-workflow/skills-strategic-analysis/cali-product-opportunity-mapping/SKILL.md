# Product Opportunity Mapping

## Goal

Identify, evaluate, and prioritize the market opportunities worth pursuing — turning market insights into actionable strategic choices.

## Domain Context

Opportunity mapping transforms market knowledge into strategic decisions. Key themes:
- **Opportunity Identification** — Where are the gaps in the market?
- **Opportunity Evaluation** — Which opportunities are worth pursuing?
- **Opportunity Prioritization** — Which should we pursue first?
- **Strategic Fit** — Does this opportunity align with our strengths?

## Opportunity Identification

### The Market Gap Framework

**1. Underserved Needs:**
- Jobs to be Done that current solutions don't address
- Pain points that customers have learned to live with
- Workarounds that reveal real problems

**2. Underserved Segments:**
- Customer groups that existing solutions ignore
- Niche markets overlooked by mass-market products
- Emerging segments not yet served

**3. Underserved Experiences:**
- Buying or onboarding experiences that are painful
- Support and service gaps
- Integration and ecosystem missing pieces

### Identifying Signals

**Market Signals:**
- Growing search volume for problem terms
- Rising complaint volumes in existing solutions
- New market segments emerging
- Regulatory or technological changes opening new space

**Customer Signals:**
- Feature requests clustering around themes
- Customer interviews revealing unaddressed needs
- Churn reasons pointing to gaps
- Usage patterns showing drop-off points

**Competitive Signals:**
- Competitors adding features in an area
- New entrants focusing on underserved space
- Market consolidation patterns
- Direct and indirect competition shifts

## Opportunity Evaluation

### The Opportunity Assessment Matrix

| Dimension | Questions | Score (1-5) |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Market Size** | How large is the addressable market? | |
| **Market Growth** | Is the market growing or shrinking? | |
| **Competitive Intensity** | How crowded is this space? | |
| **Competitive Advantage** | Can we win here sustainably? | |
| **Strategic Fit** | Does this align with our strengths? | |
| **Timing** | Is now the right time? | |

### Market Size Analysis

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):**
- The total revenue opportunity if you captured 100% of the market

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):**
- The segment you can realistically serve

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):**
- The market share you can realistically capture

**Calculation approaches:**
- **Top-down:** Start from total market, filter down
- **Bottom-up:** Start from customer acquisitions, multiply up

### Competitive Analysis

**Porter's Five Forces for Product:**

1. **Threat of New Entrants:**
   - How easy is it to enter this space?
   - What are the barriers to entry?

2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers:**
   - How much leverage do suppliers have?
   - Are there alternative suppliers?

3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers:**
   - How price-sensitive are customers?
   - Are there many alternatives?

4. **Threat of Substitutes:**
   - What else could customers use instead?
   - How compelling are alternatives?

5. **Competitive Rivalry:**
   - How intense is the competition?
   - Who are the key players?

### Timing Assessment

**Market timing factors:**
- Is the market ready? (early vs. right time vs. too late)
- Is technology ready? (enabling tech exists or emerging)
- Is regulation ready? (supportive or restrictive)
- Is the team ready? (do we have the skills and resources?)

## Opportunity Prioritization

### Prioritization Frameworks

**RICE Score:**
```
RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort
```

- **Reach:** How many users will be affected?
- **Impact:** How much will it help each user?
- **Confidence:** How sure are we about the estimates?
- **Effort:** How much time/resources to build?

**Value vs. Confidence Matrix:**

| High Value + High Confidence | High Value + Low Confidence |
|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Low Value + High Confidence** | **Low Value + Low Confidence** |

Prioritize: High Value + High Confidence first
Investigate: High Value + Low Confidence
Delegate: Low Value + High Confidence
Deprioritize: Low Value + Low Confidence

### Strategic Fit Assessment

**Core Competencies:**
- What are we uniquely good at?
- What skills and assets do we have?

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Does this opportunity support our mission?
- Does it create strategic optionality?
- Does it strengthen our ecosystem?

**Resource Requirements:**
- Do we have the resources to pursue this?
- What's the opportunity cost?

## Opportunity Development

### The Opportunity Canvas

**1. Opportunity Statement:**
- What problem are we solving?
- For whom?
- In what context?

**2. Market Evidence:**
- What signals indicate this is real?
- What's the evidence?

**3. Competitive Landscape:**
- Who addresses this today?
- How well do they serve it?
- What are the gaps?

**4. Strategic Rationale:**
- Why should we pursue this?
- How does it fit our strategy?

**5. Resource Requirements:**
- What does it take to win?
- What's the investment needed?

**6. Risks:**
- What could go wrong?
- What's our mitigation plan?

### Opportunity Roadmap

**Phase 1: Exploration (0-3 months)**
- Validate the opportunity exists
- Understand the competitive landscape
- Define success metrics

**Phase 2: Experimentation (3-6 months)**
- Test the opportunity with experiments
- Build minimum viable products
- Measure market response

**Phase 3: Expansion (6-12 months)**
- Scale what's working
- Build competitive moats
- Optimize unit economics

**Phase 4: Optimization (12+ months)**
- Deepen market penetration
- Expand use cases
- Defend against competition

## Gotchas

1. **Evidence over intuition** — Don't pursue opportunities without market evidence
2. **Timing matters** — Being right too early is often as bad as being wrong
3. **Strategic fit** — Pursue opportunities where you can win
4. **Prioritize ruthlessly** — Not all good opportunities are worth pursuing
