# Evolutionary Product Thinking

> Inspired by: evolutionary systems, novelty search, stepping-stones theory, adaptive systems, exaptation, emergent innovation.

## Goal

Help teams avoid premature convergence, identify promising stepping-stones, evaluate evolutionary potential, and think beyond rigid roadmap logic.

## When to Use

Activate when:
- Product strategy feels too objective-driven or roadmap-rigid
- Teams are prematurely optimizing toward fixed outcomes
- Exploring innovation, experimentation, or long-term adaptability
- Evaluating product direction beyond short-term metrics
- Prioritization discussions feel shallow or disconnected
- Discussing ecosystems, platforms, adaptability, or emergent behavior
- Exploring feature prioritization through connections and optionality

**Do NOT activate for:**
- Strict KPI optimization (use cali-shape-up instead)
- Deterministic roadmap generation
- Growth-at-all-costs planning

## Core Philosophy

### Evolution is Discovery, Not Goal Execution

**Instead of:**
- Rigid optimization toward predefined objectives
- Narrow feature planning
- Excessive convergence
- Deterministic roadmaps

**Encourage:**
- Exploration and novelty generation
- Adaptive discovery
- Optionality creation
- Ecosystem thinking
- Accumulation of stepping-stones

### Stepping-Stones Principle

> Important innovations often cannot be reached directly.

Evaluate ideas not only by:
- Immediate ROI / short-term impact / direct business metrics

But also by:
- What they **unlock** / **connect** / **enable**
- What future paths they reveal
- What new niches they make possible

## Process

### Step 1: Evolutionary Diagnosis

Analyze:
- Current evolutionary constraints
- Convergence risks
- Ecosystem limitations
- Adaptability bottlenecks
- Exploration quality

### Step 2: Stepping-Stones Analysis

Identify:
- Enabling capabilities
- Optionality creators
- Future unlocks
- Reusable primitives
- Latent opportunities

### Step 3: Evolutionary Forces Analysis

Evaluate the proposal through 6 forces:

| Force | In Products | Key Question |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **Selection Pressure** | Market forces, user adoption, sustainability | Are we over-optimizing too early? |
| **Mutation** | Accidental discoveries, unexpected workflows, productive "misuse" | What edge cases could become future patterns? |
| **Genetic Drift** | Small workflow shifts, architectural evolution | What subtle shifts compound into major changes? |
| **Gene Flow** | Integrations, APIs, community extensions | What external systems could introduce new behaviors? |
| **Exaptation** | Infrastructure reused, APIs becoming platforms | What future purposes could this capability serve? |
| **Local Competition** | Specialization, serving niches, differentiation | What niche are we uniquely adapted for? |

### Step 4: Genotype vs. Phenotype Analysis

**Genotype** — The adaptable underlying structure:
- Architecture, primitives, APIs, capabilities, composability, extensibility

**Phenotype** — The visible expression experienced by users:
- Workflows, templates, configurations, UI manifestations

**Strategic question:** Does the system support multiple expressions? Can users adapt it to different contexts?

### Step 5: Connection Mapping

Evaluate not only isolated feature value, but also:
- Relationship networks
- Enabling potential
- Dependency structures
- Ecosystem influence
- Adjacency effects

### Step 6: Evolutionary Prioritization

Prioritize not only by impact/effort/revenue. Also evaluate:
- Enabling power
- Network effects
- Optionality creation
- Future adaptability
- Ecosystem leverage
- Exaptation potential
- Experimentation acceleration

## Output Format

This skill produces:
- **Evolutionary diagnosis** — Constraints, risks, ecosystem limitations
- **Stepping-stones analysis** — Enabling capabilities, future unlocks
- **Forces analysis** — All 6 evolutionary forces evaluated
- **Connection map** — Feature relationships, workflow adjacency
- **Evolutionary opportunities** — Experimentation paths, future niches
- **Risks of premature convergence** — Rigidity, lock-in, narrow KPIs
- **Strategic recommendations** — Exploration directions, optionality preservation

## Gotchas

1. **Evolution ≠ growth-at-all-costs** — Avoid endless expansion, convergence obsession, extraction-only strategies
2. **Survival is not always the goal** — Some products should evolve, split, transform, merge, or disappear
3. **Metrics can blind discovery** — Excessive KPI fixation narrows exploration and eliminates stepping-stones
4. **Prefer exploration over premature optimization** — Avoid overcommitting early, rigid assumptions, excessive convergence
5. **Value enabling capabilities** — High-value features often matter more for what they unlock than direct user value
